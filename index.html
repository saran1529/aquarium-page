GUPPY fish

The guppy fish, also known as Poecilia reticulata, is a small, colorful freshwater fish that is popular among aquarium enthusiasts. Here are some details about guppy fish

Guppies are peaceful and active swimmers. They thrive in community tanks with other non-aggressive fish.
They do best in groups, typically with a male-to-female ratio of 1:2 or 1:3 to reduce stress on females.

MOLLY fish

The Molly fish (Poecilia sphenops) is another popular freshwater fish among aquarium enthusiasts, known for its peaceful nature and hardiness. They are a great choice for beginners and come in a variety of vibrant colors and patterns.

Mollies are peaceful and active swimmers, making them ideal for community tanks.
They are social and prefer to live in groups of at least 3–5. However, maintaining a male-to-female ratio of 1:2 or 1:3 helps reduce stress among females.

betta

The Betta fish (Betta splendens), also known as the Siamese fighting fish, is one of the most popular and visually stunning freshwater fish in the aquarium hobby. They are known for their vibrant colors, flowing fins, and unique personalities

Betta fish are territorial and can be aggressive, especially males.
Males: Should not be kept together as they will fight.
Females: Can live in groups (sororities) of 4–6 in a well-planted tank, but this requires careful monitoring.
Tank Mates: They are compatible with peaceful species like snails, shrimp, Corydoras, and small tetras.
Bettas are intelligent and will recognize their owners, often coming to the surface during feeding times.

glow tetra

The Glow Tetra (also known as the Glowlight Tetra) is a peaceful and vibrant freshwater fish that is perfect for beginner and community aquariums. They are known for their bright, glowing appearance and schooling behavior, adding a beautiful dynamic to tanks.

Peaceful and timid, Glow Tetras are excellent community fish.
They feel most secure and display their best colors when kept in groups of 6 or more.
Their synchronized swimming creates a mesmerizing display.

shrimps

Aquarium Shrimps are fascinating and popular additions to freshwater tanks. They are not only beautiful but also functional, as they help keep the tank clean by eating algae and leftover food. Their vibrant colors and active behavior make them a favorite among aquarists.

Shrimps are peaceful and best kept with:
Small, non-aggressive fish like Neon Tetras, Guppies, or Rasboras.
Other shrimp species with similar water requirements.
Snails like Nerite Snails or Mystery Snails.
Avoid large, predatory fish like Cichlids, Bettas, or Goldfish.

snails

Assassin Snails (Clea helena) are a fascinating and functional addition to many freshwater aquariums. These carnivorous snails are known for their ability to control pest snail populations in a tank, making them popular among aquarists.

They effectively reduce populations of nuisance snails like Pond Snails and Malaysian Trumpet Snails.
Their striped shells and burrowing behavior add visual interest to the tank.
Easy to care for and adaptable to various tank setups
